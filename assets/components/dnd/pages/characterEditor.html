Ability scores

You can get your ability scores in three different ways.


1. Asign these numbers to the abilities: 15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8

2. Roll 4d6 and remove the lowest number, then add them up. Do this 6 times total and then assign them to your abilities.

3. The third method is the point buy system. You get 27 points and all your abilities start at 8. You can then use those points to increase abilities. You can't have abilities higher than 15 with this method and lower than 8. So as an example: if you want strength as your highest score, you can spend 9 point to kick strength up to 15. Now you have 18 points left. You could then put 6 points in charisma with then becomes 14. Then you only have 12 points left. Do this until your points are up

Ability modifiers

To calculate your abiltiy modifiers, you take your (ability score - 10) / 2 rounded down.

Proficiency bonus

Your proficiency bonus can be found in the 'proficiency' colom of your classes table.

Saving throws

Saving throws modifiers are your ability modifiers plus your proficiency bonus if you are proficient in a saving throw. For example, if my dexterity modifier is +3 and my proficieny is +2, my dexterity saving throw becomes +5.

Skills

You can fill these in like you did with the saving throws.

Passive perception

This is 10 + your perception skill.

Initiative

This is your dexterity modifier.
